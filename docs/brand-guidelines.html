Brand Guidelines · v1.1 · 2026

SalesRx

Walk in already knowing. SalesRx arms sales reps with intelligence before every meeting — the brand must feel like what it delivers: strength, courage, and seriousness. A briefing room, not a party.

1 · Brand idea

The rep's corner team. Like a fighter's corner: it studies the opponent, knows your strengths, and hands you the game plan before the bell. Every design decision should pass one test: does this make a rep feel more prepared and more confident walking into a room?

Brand attributes

Strong — grounded, stable, never flimsy. Courageous — decisive accents, direct language, no hedging. Serious — restrained, precise, data-first; confidence without noise.

2 · Color system

The palette is built on documented color psychology: deep navy signals authority, competence and calm control (the "navy suit" effect); deep emerald green signals GO — growth, money, decisive forward motion (courage without the alarm-bell baggage red carries in software); restrained gold signals achievement. Red is reserved exclusively for warnings. Dark, low-saturation grounds keep the tone serious — bright accents are earned, not decorative.

Green loses its authority when overused — one primary action per view. Red appears only when something needs attention; gold marks results, never navigation. Both accents must always sit on navy/midnight grounds, never on each other.

Accessibility

Paper on Command Navy: 13.8:1 ✓ · White on Vanguard Green: 4.7:1 ✓ · Paper on Valor Red: 5.9:1 ✓ · Midnight on Medal Gold: 8.6:1 ✓. For small green text on dark grounds use the bright tint #3DDC97, not #0E8C55. Red and green fills never sit adjacent (colorblind safety) — separate with navy or paper.

Clear space & minimums

Clear space around the mark = height of the "R". Minimum sizes: shield 24px, chevron 16px, wordmark 90px wide. On photos, always place the mark on a navy scrim — never raw on imagery.

4 · Typography

One family, many weights: Inter (free, variable, excellent at small data sizes). Weight — not color — carries hierarchy: 900 for display, 700 for headings, 400–500 for body. Numerals in briefs use tabular figures.

6 · Voice & tone

Like a trusted corner coach: direct, brief, specific, calm under pressure. Never hype, never hedge.

We think there might possibly be some interesting signals worth exploring! 🎉Three signals this week. The strongest: their VP of Supply Chain left Monday.
Our revolutionary AI-powered next-gen platform leverages cutting-edge technology…You'll know their stack, their pain, and your opening question — before you park the car.
Oops! Something went wrong somewhere :(Research failed: calendar feed unreachable. Check CALENDAR_ICS_URL and retry.

7 · Rules of engagement

DO
  • Keep green for the one action that matters on each screen
  • Use red only when something demands attention (warnings, destructive actions)
  • Use navy depth (not drop shadows) to layer surfaces
  • Cite sources next to every claim — precision is the brand
  • Use gold only when the rep gained something (score, win, alert confirmed)
  • Write button labels as verbs: "Research", "Prep brief", "Sync"
DON'T
  • No gradients on brand marks; flat fills only
  • No emoji in headings or CTAs (fine in docs/changelogs)
  • No green-on-gold, red-on-green, or gold-on-red combinations, ever
  • No rounded "friendly" blob shapes — geometry stays angular
  • No exclamation points in product copy
